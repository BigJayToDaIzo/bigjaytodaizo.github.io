Good evening everyone!  I'm pretty excited to finally have a blog up and going on Github Pages.

I don't have a wild amount of time to spend on this particular Hello World post, but I feel like for a first post I have to say SOMETHING of significance.  

The very day I started working on Transient Tracker back in August of 2017, I knew immediately that I wanted to blog about my adventures.  There has been SO MUCH that I've learned!  Following is a very small, non-comprehensive list:

1. College didn't prepare me for what I've found myself involved in.

2. Computer Science is not about learning how to program in C, Java, Python or any other language.  It turns out most of the work I've done is learning how to make technologies communicate with one another!

3. Most projects aren't written in a single language.  Most projects consist of an "Environment Stack".  Or a stack of languages, technologies and frameworks that all work together to solve a specific problem.

4. I spend a LOT of time doing everything BUT programming.  

5. Soft skills are like GODMODE in this industry.  If you can interact easily with other people (especially the typically introverted STEM professional) you will go far.

With all that in mind, I would like to invite you to follow me on twitter (link is in header), and I promise to make regular updates to this blog and keep everyone in the loop on what I'm working on and the progress I'm making.  Expect to see pics and code snippets and all kinds of cool stuff!

But for now, follow all those social media links in the header and please feel free to offer your constructive criticisms as well!

OH YEAH, there's only 2 rules here.
1. No Assholes
2. No dying

Keep looking up my friends!
